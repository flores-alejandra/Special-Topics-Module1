Research Reflection

In this first deliverable, one big challenge was understanding how to use the action research cycle properly. The planning and acting parts went well, we set goals, brainstormed, and researched. But the observing and reflecting steps happened too late, mostly in the last two weeks. Because of that, we could not polish the interactions as much as we wanted. If we had done the full cycle sooner, we would have spotted problems earlier and pitch a more solid idea to our peers. The main takeaway is that repeating the action research cycle often, and starting it early, makes a huge difference. Doing it just once at the end meant we missed chances to keep improving. Now I see why doing observation and reflection quickly is so important: it keeps curiosity alive, builds knowledge fast, and helps the project grow stronger. For the next deliverable, I want to do at least two full cycles, plan and act on small parts, observe how they work with quick tests, reflect right away, and adjust. This should help us define the user interactions much better and end up with a polished prototype.
